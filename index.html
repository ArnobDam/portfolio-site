About Me

I am a programmer whose love for computers started in elementary school when I would kill time playing Windows games like 3D Pinball and Spider Solitaire.

Now, I a 24-year old software engineer who has programmed both for work and as an outlet to explore my passion for new technology. I worked as a software engineer for the US Air Force on a two-year contract as my first job out of college. Before that, I graduated Washington University in St. Louis in 2020, where I studied Systems Engineering & Computer Science.

Symphonify

Symphonify is a pixel-perfect clone of Spotify's web player. I created Symphonify using React / Redux for the front-end, Ruby on Rails for the back-end, a PostgreSQL database, and AWS S3 to store and access mp3 and image files.

Express (Point-of-Sale)

Express is a Point-of-Sale system built upon efficiency, ease of access and sleek visuals. Express uses the MERN Stack. As project lead of a group of four software engineers, I contributed to both the front- and back-end of our system, set the day-to-day schedules for all team members, and lead our team meeetings.

Gorillas

Gorillas is a projectile motion game coded via Javascript, HTML & CSS. The goal of the game is to throw a banana and hit the AI gorilla before he hits you! The banana's path implements projectile motion physics, vector math and trigonometry.
